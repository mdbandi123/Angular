Voice cloning a form of deepfake

By Aenon Santiago, April 29, 2022

“Deepfake” is a term that was by a Reddit user that shares the same name. The said Reddit user used the technology to create a space on the online news and aggregation site, where they colleagues exchange, shares pornographic video materials that used a form of an AI technology which is called deep learning, with the help of the said AI you will be able to create an uncanny image or a video that switches the original likeness of a person with another person. The technology kept evolving since then, from photos to videos and now to audios.
Deepfake voice, it is also called as synthetic voice or voice cloning, this technology uses the same deep learning AI to generate a clone of a real person’s voice. The said technology frighteningly advanced and come to a point where the generated voice replicated the human voice with a great amount of accuracy in likeness and tone.

The emergence of a technology like this has many worried about how it can be implemented to manipulate and affect a broad range of things from political discourse. Some warning signs have already surface and it take the form of a phone scam together with fake videos of celebrities on social medias of which they claimed they never did in the first place.

Along with rise of the deepfake technology are the ways that can be implemented to analyze or detect if a video is genuine. Nina Schick come up with a two-ways of approaching the deepfake problem. The first approach is to build a technology that counters deepfake technology in a way that it will be able to determine if a video has been manipulated, “This is always going to be a game of cat and mouse”, Schick said. “Because as fast as you built the detection model that can counter and determine a deepfake, there will always be a generator that will be able to overcome that detector”. The second approach is to validate real videos known as “media provenance”. To execute this, technology will need to be embedded in hardware and software. Think of this in a way as a digital watermark that is imprinted every time a video is edited/manipulated.
