Understanding ‘Deepfakes’

By Miguel Tiamzon, May 01, 2022

Deepfakes are exploited video content or other virtual representations created by sophisticated artificial intelligence that result in falsified images and sounds that seem to be real. It is a type of artificial intelligence that combines the terms "deep learning" and "fake." Deep learning is defined as a "subset of AI," and it refers to algorithms that can learn and make intelligent decisions on their own. However, the risk is that "technology can be used to make people believe something is real when it is not," according to Peter Singer, a cybersecurity and defense-focused strategist and senior fellow at the New America think tank.

By studying photographs and videos of a target person from various angles and then mimicking their behavior and speech patterns, a deep-learning system can create a convincing counterfeit. Through the use of a type of technology called ‘machine learning’, it can create a digital version of someone. Amateur deepfakes can be created with apps or programs, but they can usually be identified by unusual flickering or blurring around someone's face. Deepfake is surely considered as an advancement in our technology today. Although there are benefits to using deepfakes for productive purposes, the negative effects of deepfakes are easy to imagine and frightening to contemplate.
