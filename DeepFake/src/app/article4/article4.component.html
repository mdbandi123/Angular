Best Ways to Prevent Deepfakes

By Sam Manalo, April 30, 2022

The name Deepfake" is derived from the underlying AI technique of deep learning. Deep learning algorithms are u sed to swap faces in video and digital information to create realistic-looking false media. Deep learning algorithms train themselves how to solve issues when given vast volumes of data. However, how can we avoid it?

There are a few ways to avoid it, the first of which is the legal measures. There are currently no substantial measures in place to protect persons from deepfaking or faked voice recordings. Increasing the costs of creating and disseminating (or assisting) fraudulent material by imposing severe penalties on the behavior will function as a deterrent to improper uses of technology. The next task is to train your computers to recognize fakes. Some of the current imperfect deepfakes may now be identified using evident antiquities or heuristic analysis. Microsoft has unveiled a new method for detecting faults in synthetic media. The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency, or DARPA, is working on a software called SemaFor that aims to detect semantic flaws in deepfakes, such as an image of a guy generated with anatomically incorrect teeth or a person wearing socially unusual jewelry. Last but not least, the use of blockchain technology might be a solution. A distributed ledger, or blockchain, allows you to store data online without the use of centralized servers. Furthermore, blockchains are resistant to a wide range of security vulnerabilities that centralized data storage are susceptible to. Although distributed ledgers are not currently capable of storing large amounts of data, they are suitable for storing hashes and digital signatures.

It's critical to educate people about AI algorithms' amazing powers. This can help users become more aware of emerging technologies like deepfakes, which can help avoid, at least to some extent, the development of problematic programs like FakeApp.
